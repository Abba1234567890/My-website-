HISTORY

Junior secondary school wada Abubakar was founded on the 18th of october 2004, marking the beginning of a new era in education for the community. With an initial enrollment of 206 students, the school commenced its the stewardship operations under the of mr. Ali bashir as the principal, alongside mr. Isyaku umar serving as the vice principal and Mr. Dan Azumi Ya'u as the senior master. situated at the modern Qur'anic primary school Kiyawa as its temporary site, the school functioned across four classes: class la with 52 students,Class 15 with 52 students, Class le with 51 students Class Id with 51 students. :

This arrangement brought the total student population to 206, reflecting the immediate impact and importance of the school in the community. in 2012, a significant milestone was achieved when the school management successfully transitioned to its permanent site at Sabon Gari Kiyawa. This relocation was made possible through the generous allocation of land by the local government chairman, Alhaji Dahiru Madawaki Katuka, further solidifying the school's presence and commitment to education in the area. Over the years, junior secondary school Wada Abubakar has witnessed a succession of dedicated leaders who have guided its growth and development
